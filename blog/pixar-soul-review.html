Review: Pixar's Soul

February 8, 2024 Movie Review 7 min read

Pixar's Soul is a masterful exploration of what it means to be alive, wrapped in a visually stunning package that only animation could deliver. Released in 2020, this film tackles existential questions with the grace and sophistication that has become Pixar's signature.

The Premise and Its Brilliance

Soul follows Joe Gardner, a middle-school music teacher with dreams of becoming a jazz musician. When he finally gets his big break, an accident sends his soul to the "Great Before," where unborn souls develop their personalities before entering Earth. The film's premise is deceptively simple but allows for profound exploration of life's biggest questions.

Visual Storytelling at Its Finest

The film's visual design is nothing short of spectacular, with two distinct worlds that feel both familiar and otherworldly:

  • New York City: Rendered with such detail and warmth that it feels like a love letter to the city
  • The Great Before: A minimalist, abstract space that perfectly captures the concept of pre-existence
  • The Zone: A surreal, flowing environment that represents the state of complete absorption in an activity
  • Character design: The souls are simple, glowing figures that convey emotion through color and movement

Jazz as a Metaphor for Life

The choice of jazz as Joe's passion is particularly brilliant. Jazz, with its improvisation, collaboration, and emphasis on feeling over perfection, becomes a perfect metaphor for life itself. The film's soundtrack, composed by Trent Reznor and Atticus Ross with jazz arrangements by Jon Batiste, is a character in its own right.

Existential Themes Handled with Care

Soul tackles some of life's biggest questions without ever feeling heavy-handed:

  • Purpose vs. passion: The difference between what we're meant to do and what we love to do
  • The meaning of success: Whether achieving our dreams brings the fulfillment we expect
  • Being present: The importance of appreciating life's small moments
  • Death and the afterlife: Handled with sensitivity and without religious overtones

Character Development and Voice Acting

The Film's Cultural Significance

Soul is significant for several reasons:

  • Representation: Features Pixar's first Black protagonist in a lead role
  • Mature themes: Proves that animation can tackle complex philosophical questions
  • Accessibility: Makes profound ideas accessible to audiences of all ages
  • Artistic innovation: Pushes the boundaries of what animated films can achieve

The Animation's Technical Achievement

The film's technical accomplishments are worth noting:

  • Lighting: The way light interacts with different materials and environments is groundbreaking
  • Character animation: Subtle facial expressions and body language that convey deep emotion
  • Abstract sequences: The Zone sequences use animation techniques that would be impossible in live-action
  • Color theory: Each environment has its own distinct color palette that reflects its emotional purpose

Life Lessons That Resonate

Beyond its entertainment value, Soul offers genuine wisdom about living a meaningful life:

  • Success isn't always what we think it will be
  • Life's purpose might be found in the journey, not the destination
  • Small moments of joy are what make life worth living
  • Being present is more important than being perfect

Final Thoughts

Soul is a rare film that manages to be both entertaining and profound, accessible to children but meaningful to adults. It's a reminder that animation, at its best, can explore the human condition with a depth and nuance that live-action films often struggle to achieve.

The film's message about finding joy in life's simple pleasures is particularly relevant in our fast-paced, achievement-oriented world. It's a gentle reminder to slow down, appreciate what we have, and find meaning in the everyday moments that make up our lives.

Rating: 5/5

A beautiful, thoughtful film that proves animation can tackle the deepest questions of human existence with grace and artistry.
