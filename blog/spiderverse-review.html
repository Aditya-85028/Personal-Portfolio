Review: Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse

April 15, 2024 Movie Review 8 min read

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse isn't just another superhero movie—it's a revolutionary piece of animation that redefines what the medium can achieve. Released in 2018, this film broke new ground in visual storytelling and proved that animation can be as sophisticated and emotionally resonant as any live-action film.

The Visual Revolution

What immediately strikes you about Into the Spider-Verse is its groundbreaking visual style. The film combines traditional 3D computer animation with comic book aesthetics, creating a unique hybrid that feels both modern and nostalgic. The animators at Sony Pictures Animation took inspiration from comic book panels, using techniques like:

  • Halftone dots: Mimicking the printing process of comic books
  • Split-screen panels: Breaking the frame into comic-style layouts
  • Onomatopoeic text: Integrating sound effects directly into the visual design
  • Variable frame rates: Using different animation speeds to create dynamic movement

Storytelling That Transcends Age

At its core, Into the Spider-Verse is a coming-of-age story about Miles Morales, a young Afro-Latino teenager who discovers he has spider powers. But the film goes beyond the typical superhero origin story by exploring themes of:

  • Identity and belonging: Miles struggles with his dual heritage and finding his place in the world
  • Legacy and responsibility: The weight of living up to the Spider-Man mantle
  • Family relationships: Complex dynamics with his father, a police officer, and his uncle, who becomes the villain
  • Multiverse consequences: The impact of one person's choices across different realities

Character Development and Representation

The film's diverse cast of Spider-People from different dimensions is more than just a gimmick—it's a celebration of representation in superhero media. Each character brings their own unique perspective and struggles:

  • Peter B. Parker: A jaded, divorced version of Spider-Man who learns to believe in himself again
  • Gwen Stacy: A confident, ballet-trained Spider-Woman with her own tragic backstory
  • Spider-Ham: A cartoon pig version that adds humor while still being integral to the plot
  • Peni Parker: A young girl who pilots a robot, representing anime influences
  • Spider-Man Noir: A black-and-white version from the 1930s, adding film noir elements

Technical Innovation

The technical achievements of this film cannot be overstated. The animation team developed new techniques to achieve the comic book aesthetic:

  • Line work: Hand-drawn lines were added to give characters a more organic, comic book feel
  • Color separation: Using different color layers to create depth and texture
  • Motion blur alternatives: Instead of traditional motion blur, they used techniques like multiple images and strobing effects
  • Sound design: The soundtrack by Daniel Pemberton perfectly complements the visual style

Cultural Impact

Into the Spider-Verse didn't just succeed commercially and critically—it changed the animation industry. Its success proved that:

  • Audiences are ready for more diverse superhero stories
  • Animation can tackle mature themes without being "just for kids"
  • Innovative visual styles can be commercially successful
  • Comic book adaptations can be faithful to their source material while being cinematic

Legacy and Influence

The film's influence can be seen in subsequent animated projects, from The Mitchells vs. the Machines to Puss in Boots: The Last Wish. It proved that there's an audience for bold, experimental animation that doesn't follow the traditional Disney/Pixar formula.

Final Thoughts

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse is more than just a great animated film—it's a landmark achievement that pushes the boundaries of what animation can accomplish. It's a film that respects its audience, regardless of age, and delivers a story that's both entertaining and meaningful.

The film's success lies in its perfect balance of innovation and accessibility. It's visually stunning enough to impress animation enthusiasts, emotionally resonant enough to move general audiences, and thematically rich enough to reward multiple viewings.

Rating: 5/5

A masterpiece of animation that redefines the superhero genre and proves that animation is a medium for all ages.
